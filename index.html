The Challenge

Small and marginal farmers in India face significant challenges in making informed agricultural decisions. They rely heavily on traditional knowledge, local shopkeepers, or guesswork for critical decisions like crop selection, pest control, and fertilizer usage.

This dependency leads to poor yields, excessive input costs, and environmental degradation due to chemical overuse. Language barriers and low digital literacy further limit their access to modern agricultural technology.
